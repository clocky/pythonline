Shortly thereafter, 'Spamme Clubs' did appear up and down the country where men might gather to take a glass of coffee and discuss the weighty matters of the day and the origin of the mysterious tinne of spamme. Its health was drunk after the port at dinner, it was denounced by the clergy as a blasphemy, the Kind sent for it and apparently dined with it, for Spamme shirts with 'By Royal Appointment' appeared in the Mall, where they were hawked for pennies.
