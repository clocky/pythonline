Eric's Favorite Books of 1996

For many of you who like to read I thought I'd give you a selection of my favorite books of 1996. These are the books I most enjoyed last year, though not all of them were newly published in the year. Hope you enjoy them.

Napoleon and Josephine
Evangeline Bruce
My book of the year. A wonderfully descriptive social history of France through the Revolution and the Empire. Sadly this was her only book but it is fabulous, the sort of book you dread finishing. History has never been so alive, or the shit Napoleon so brilliantly skewered. He is like a little mob chieftain from Sicily. Thoroughly recommended.

Putting On The Ritz
Joe Keenan
Hilarious. The funniest book I read all year. This beautifully written comedy about two New York men and their female friend, who become involved in chaotic infighting between two rich feuding publishers - one clearly the Donald (Trump) and the other a seventy year old Malcolm Forbes. There are many truly funny set pieces and inspired dialogue. I sent this to several friends and I recommend you rush out and read it.

What Am I Doing Here?
Bruce Chatwin
Wonderful travel pieces. He writes so well. What a great writer.

Coming Through Slaughter
Michael Ondaatje
I love the way he writes. This is the story of Buddy Bolden, a New Orleans jazz trumpeter who went nuts while playing in New Orleans. Beautifully written, almost like a scenario, he brings everything to life. Those who have only read The English Patient may well enjoy reading more of this wonderful writer.
