Flash forward later in the year and we are filming somewhere in Shepherds Bush, in the mean little back streets behind the BBC. We are set to shoot this gag. I am wearing a silly wig (as usual) hiding my shoulder length hair. Our cameraman has done lots of documentaries and he knows exactly the way the gag should work. The first take works fine. "From these streets" I begin walking slowly with the camera "very near the Cavern Rutland" - the camera car increases speed and I am forced to increase my pace to keep up with it, "Dirk, Nasty, Stig and Barry, the legendary pre-fab Four, the legendary Rutles.." the camera pulls away, I break into a run. I end up hurtling down the street yelling at full speed whilst the camera-car disappears round the corner. We do it again a couple of times but I know we've got it first take. It seemed really funny running up the street after a fast disappearing film crew. I had a strange little memory of my Provencal hideaway.

A few days later we are down in Shepperton Studios filming I Must Be in Love the song Neil had sent me, only now we are dressed as sixties Beatles and it has become a song from A Hard Days Rut. It's November, it's freezing, it's intermittently raining and we are making up sight gags in a field that has been a housing estate for almost twenty years now. Neil is Nasty, David Batley is Dirk, I am shaking a leg as George (Stig) and John Halsey is the lovable Barry.
